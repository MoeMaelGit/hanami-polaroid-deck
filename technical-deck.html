Hanami Polaroid

A standalone VR photography experience for Meta Quest. Walk a Japanese garden in bloom, shoot through a living viewfinder, and develop polaroids you hang on the line.

XR Class & Design · Carulli Unity 6 · URP · OpenXR Meta Quest 2 / 3
Concept

Photography is the gameplay

Hanami (花見) is the contemplative ritual of watching the cherry blossoms. The whole loop is built to reward looking and framing rather than speed.

PILLAR 01

A real camera

Diegetic controls, not an abstract UI overlay: a viewfinder you look through, with physical zoom, focus, and aperture.

PILLAR 02

Tangible photos

Shots eject as physical polaroids you shake to develop and hang. Each one becomes a spatial memory.

PILLAR 03

Comfort first

No nausea, no stereo conflicts, no unwanted motion. Treated as a hard requirement, not polish.

The experience

One loop, guided then free

Grab camera→ Stand in a ring→ Spawn & mount tripod→ Enter viewfinder (B)→ Aim · zoom · focus→ Shutter→ Polaroid ejects→ Shake to develop→ Hang on the line→ Roam to the next spot
  • A guided tutorial reads live game state (so its prompts are always truthful) and walks you to your first developed, hung photo.
  • Then free-roam opens across six rings around the garden; tripods spawn facing the central scenery.
System architecture

A camera inside a camera

Player ▸ XR Origin rig ├─ HanamiCamera (grabbable) │ ├─ VirtualCamera ─▶ RT_Viewfinder │ ├─ on-body LCD (samples the RT) │ ├─ ViewfinderRenderer (on while held) │ └─ DirectGrabOnlyFilter ├─ TripodZoneController │ ├─ ZoneRing × N (terrain-conforming) │ └─ HanamiTripod ▸ CameraMountPoint ├─ CameraViewController │ └─ ViewfinderVolume (DoF · layer 8) ├─ PolaroidPrinter ▸ Polaroid ▸ Develop ├─ PhotoGallery · TutorialGuide └─ TeleportRayDisabler
  • Layer-8 isolation (ViewfinderUI): every surface that shows the feed is excluded from the secondary camera's culling mask.
  • One mechanism solves the infinite mirror and keeps UI out of photos.
  • Asset-free artefacts: polaroids are generated entirely in code.
Pipeline

From lens to LCD

VirtualCameramono render → RT_Viewfinder 1024×768→ world-space rear-LCD canvas→ your eyes
  • The framed view is rendered monoscopically, because a photograph is a flat image, not a stereo pair.
  • Shown on a rear-LCD canvas: bezel, live feed, and a readout of focal · f-stop · distance.
  • The canvas is un-mirrored at the root (negated X scale) so text and image read correctly while facing the eyes.
  • Secondary camera runs only while the camera is held, so there's no idle render cost.
The simulated lens

One physical lens, not four knobs

AIMLeft stick · re-asserted each frame, zero drift
ZOOMFOV 60° → 12°  (≈ 21 to 115 mm · 24 mm full-frame)
FOCUSLogarithmic 0.5 → 200 m
APERTUREf/1.8 → f/22 (standard stops)
SHUTTERFlash + synthesized click + haptic

Focal length for the Bokeh blur is derived from the live FOV, so zoom and depth-of-field are one consistent lens, and the blur stays physically correct at every step.

f = sensorHeight / (2 · tan(FOV / 2))

Right-stick zoom (Y) and focus (X) use dominant-axis gating, so a diagonal push never cross-talks.

Depth of field

Honest optics, honest feedback

  • URP Bokeh blurs near and far, like a real lens, and runs only on the viewfinder texture.
  • A red→green focus dot + centre reticle, computed from the physical depth of field.
  • Capture is not gated on focus, so deliberately soft shots are allowed.
hyperfocal H = f² / (N · c)

N = f-number · c = circle of confusion (full-frame). Near/far sharp limits resolve around the focus distance; the band narrows as you zoom in.

VR rendering · 1 of 2

Bugs you only feel in stereo

01

The infinite mirror

The feed showed the on-body LCD, which shows the feed → runaway recursion. Fix: a ViewfinderUI layer excluded from the secondary camera's culling mask, so it films the garden, not the screens.

02

The one-eye ring (the "eye-strain" bug)

The custom neon ring rendered in the left eye only → binocular rivalry, headaches. The hand-written shader was missing the Single Pass Instanced stereo macros. Fix: add the stereo macros → renders in both eyes.

VR rendering · 2 of 2

Comfort is a rendering decision

03

Depth of field, on the eyes

Blurring the player's actual vision is instant nausea. Fix: the DoF volume lives on layer 8 and is sampled only by the secondary camera, while the eye camera has post off. The photo blurs; you never do.

04

The teleport-ray leak

The rig re-activates its teleport ray on right-stick-forward, the same stick used for zoom. Fix: re-assert the teleport interactors inactive every frame before render, so the ray can never draw.

05

Aim drift

Naive stick-aim accumulated float drift and slid the camera off its mount. Fix: re-derive the pose each frame from a captured pivot, currentRot · Inverse(Euler(pitch,yaw,0)), which keeps the clamp absolute.

Artefacts

A photo you can hold

  • On shutter, the render texture is read into a Texture2D and composited into a procedural 4:3 card, with no imported model.
  • Ejects horizontally + up-pop so it clears the tripod at any pitch.
  • ContinuousDynamic collision stops thin cards tunnelling through sloped terrain.
  • Ejects blank, develops 0→1 by shaking (card acceleration) plus a passive timer. FIFO cap of 10; each card frees its own runtime texture.
World

Clothesline & garden

PHOTO GALLERY

Catenary clothesline

True sag curves strung between the torii beams. Photos hang by their top-middle on a HingeJoint, so they keep gravity and swivel freely about Y. Left-trigger remote-stores a held photo; stored cards are exempt from the FIFO cull.

ENVIRONMENT

Borrowed scenery

Vendor garden trimmed 7×7 → 3×3 (~180×180 m, ~9.5 m relief); ~25M grass instances removed. Distant scenery is a panoramic HDRI skybox plus ground skirt and fog (借景 shakkei), not geometry. A procedural populator blends the neighbour terrain with a three-term height formula, and a sneaky Mathf.SmoothStep ≠ GLSL bug there was fixed with a custom Smooth01 helper.

Honest limitations & next steps

What's left, and what broke

Tripod slope leg-levelling: prototyped (cluster legs by azimuth, splay each onto terrain) but the solver mis-grouped parts, so it was reverted. Safer redesign: scale only the lower leg segment.
Top-down map PiP: deferred, because a second render pass costs on Quest.
Hand/head culling in the feed: a known follow-up via the same layer mechanism.
Final on-device performance numbers: pending the Android build.
